Re-Brand

Spring Soirée

V

OVERVIEW:

Collaborating with the Human Resources Student's Association (HRSA), which is a student organization at Simon Fraser University's Beedie School of Business, I functioned as their main designer for 2 months, working on their pillar event, Spring Soirée. HRSA's annual stand-up networking event, Spring Soirée, explores topics surrounding Human Resources.

Tools:

  • -Adobe Photoshop
  • -Adobe Illustrator

Skills:

  • -Team Collaboration
  • -Flexibility
  • -Adaptability

Creative:

  • -Illustration
  • -Problem Solving
  • -Iterations

PROCESS:

The club provided me with their previous logos from 2012 and 2016, and I was given the task to re-brand! I roughly sketched out some initial designs, questioning if I should go for either a fancy or modern approach.

Putting some of my sketches through Adobe Illustrator, I began to experiment with different letter arrangements and colours. At first, I used Spring Soirée's main orange colour as I wanted to ensure I maintained consistent branding with the event. However, I went onto HRSA's website and found that I really enjoyed their colour palette and took inspiration from that.

After recieving feedback on the first logo iteration, the club found that it was not on-brand for their Spring Soirée event because the main colour is orange; the blue one matched to just HRSA. Furthermore, the logo was deemed too simple and modern for Spring Soirée as they wanted something fancier, to not just encapsulate the business side of the event but also the Soirée.

Taking all the feedback they gave me, I found inspiration online while browsing through fancy logos and created an icon that captures the season of Spring and its liveliness. However, I soon realized that this logo was not scalable and the detailing in the leaves would disappear as the scale decreased, therefore I had to find a way to simplify everything.

CHALLENGES:

Communicating with such a large club was very difficult in terms of creating a finalize logo design for their event. I had to wait at least 3 weeks before the entire club could send me any feedback in regards to the logo because core members would not be available.

This caused coordinators to not meet deliverable deadlines, which heavily affected my work as I could not design without their content. Therefore, I took the initiative to create designs prior to the deadlines with the information I had.

REFLECTION:

Overall, my involvement with the Human Resources Student's Association has taught me a valuable lesson to not only iterate through multiple designs within a short time frame, but as well as being able to produce multiple ideas, learning to not be attached to a single concept.
